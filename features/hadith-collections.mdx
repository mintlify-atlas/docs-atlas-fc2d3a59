## Hadith grading

Understand the authenticity level of each hadith:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Sahih (Authentic)">
    The highest level of authenticity. The hadith has:
    - Complete and reliable chain of narrators
    - No contradictions with stronger evidence
    - Trusted narrators with sound memory
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Hasan (Good)">
    Good authenticity but slightly less than Sahih:
    - Reliable chain with minor weaknesses
    - Acceptable for deriving Islamic rulings
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Da'if (Weak)">
    Contains weaknesses in the chain or text:
    - May have unknown narrators
    - Used for encouragement, not rulings
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Mawdu' (Fabricated)">
    Fabricated or forged narrations:
    - Not authentic
    - Should not be attributed to the Prophet ﷺ
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>
  Grading is displayed with color coding - green for Sahih, yellow for Hasan, orange for Da'if, and red for Mawdu'.
</Note>
